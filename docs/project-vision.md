# EarlyLearner Project Vision

EarlyLearner is an intentional home education management app for Australian parents who want to support their preschool child before kindy/school.

The app should be very easy to use, but powerful underneath. Parents should be able to plan structured early-years learning, deliver activities at home, capture evidence, track progress, and understand whether their child is becoming school ready across the whole child, not just academics.

## Product Promise

EarlyLearner helps Australian parents plan, deliver, track, and assess an intentional early-years learning program at home, with clear school-readiness guidance and simple daily workflows.

This is not just a diary. It is a structured but parent-friendly home learning system that turns planned activities and everyday moments into evidence of growth.

## Core Product Idea

Parents should not need to become teachers or fill in heavy forms. The app should help them run a thoughtful early-years program without making home feel like school.

EarlyLearner supports both intentional activities and everyday learning moments:

- weekly learning intentions
- term themes
- planned activities
- routines and life skills
- reading together
- counting snacks
- drawing, cutting, painting, and building
- asking questions
- playing outside
- sharing toys
- following routines
- getting dressed
- helping cook or clean
- managing emotions
- talking about the world

EarlyLearner should help parents plan these experiences, carry them out, capture what happened, understand what the evidence shows, and decide what to do next.

## Core MVP

The MVP should focus on the smallest useful version of the product: intentional planning, daily use, evidence capture, progress tracking, and school-readiness guidance.

### MVP Loop

1. Plan: show today's structured focus, activity, or intention.
2. Do: guide the parent through a simple home learning activity.
3. Capture: let the parent log what happened in under 30 seconds.
4. Map: connect the evidence to learning and readiness areas.
5. Progress: show what is emerging, growing, or confident.
6. Next step: suggest what to try next based on gaps, strengths, interests, and upcoming goals.

The MVP should answer:

- What are we intentionally working on this week?
- What can we try today?
- What did my child do?
- What does this show?
- Is my child becoming school ready?
- What should we gently encourage next?

## Australian School Readiness

The readiness tracker should be designed for Australian early learning and school transition expectations.

School readiness must be a profile, not a pass/fail score. It should include:

- communication and language
- social confidence
- emotional regulation
- independence and self-care
- life skills
- character and habits
- fine motor skills
- gross motor skills
- early literacy
- early numeracy
- curiosity and problem solving
- routines and attention
- wellbeing

The language should be supportive and non-judgemental:

- emerging
- growing
- confident
- needs more opportunities

Example readiness snapshot:

- Communication: Confident
- Social play: Growing
- Independence: Emerging
- Early literacy: Growing
- Early numeracy: Confident
- Fine motor: Needs more opportunities
- Routines: Growing

## UX Principles

EarlyLearner should not feel like admin software. It should feel like a calm parent companion.

- quick daily use
- simple language
- low-pressure tracking
- minimal typing
- strong guidance underneath
- parent-friendly summaries
- clear progress without anxiety
- practical next steps
- structured planning without heavy admin

Potential UX features:

- big "Log a moment" action
- quick activity capture
- quick observation capture
- smart tags like counting, sharing, drawing, asking why, emotions, independence
- weekly recap
- child interests timeline
- readiness snapshot
- suggested activities based on recent observations
- portfolio export for kindy/school transition
- voice-to-note support later

### Life Skills, Character, And Habits

EarlyLearner should track more than academic readiness. It should help parents intentionally build practical life skills, character, and healthy habits.

Life skills may include:

- dressing
- toileting
- packing a bag
- opening lunch containers
- cleaning up
- helping prepare food
- crossing roads safely
- asking for help
- following routines
- caring for belongings

Character and habits may include:

- kindness
- patience
- persistence
- confidence
- responsibility
- gratitude
- courage
- curiosity
- sharing
- listening
- turn-taking
- emotional regulation
- independence

Habit tracking should be light and encouraging. The app should help parents notice patterns and create repeated opportunities, not turn childhood into a checklist.

Parents should be able to track:

- community events
- playgroups

- group excursions

Community records should include:

- location

This matters because school readiness includes social confidence, communication, independence, routines, and group participation, not only home-based activities.
